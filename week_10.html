Week 10

by Charles Incorvia

Posted on April 2, 2017

The Tenth Blog Post!

What did you do this past week?
This week was spent setting up the lab, mostly. I've made good progress so far - the code (minus some serious bugfixing) for all of the base classes is comeplete. After the debugging process has been done, all that remains is to read and print the input and output effectively. That could be an easy process, like it was in the allocator lab, or it could be a difficult one, like it was in the Voting lab.

What will you do next week?
The coming week will be mostly spent completing the lab. With the bulk of the project done, this shouldn't be a huge problem, but I am often surprised how long simple things can take in C++. It only take one subtle bug to bog down a whole project!

What's in the way?
Other classes, projects, and interviewing are the biggest things in the way. Despite the rest of the lab being fairly simple it will still take time, which there seems to be less of in the coming week.

What's my experience in the class?
Working with a partner certainly helped on the last lab. I was lucky enough to have a consistent schedule the last couple of weeks so that it was feasible, and the whole lab was quite a bit less frustrating and there were way fewer bugs to deal with. Unfortunately this time I'm back on my own.

What's the tip of the week?
Hofstadter's law: Everything in programming always takes longer than you expect, even if you account for Hofstadter's law. I've come to discover it's very true when I estimate times for the labs. If I overestimate time for debugging and testing, it takes way longer. I've given up estimating logically and just make wild guesses now, and I'm getting quite a bit closer.
